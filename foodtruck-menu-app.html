FoodTruck Menu App

Food Truck Menu App allows to search for food trucks in the area, view their menu, as well as obtain information about the calorific value of meals, nutrients and allergens. It also allows you to order meals without queuing.

The goal

Design an app which allows find food truck with the rich menu close to user..

Target audience

People who want to keep fit and take care of nutrients in their meals. Additionally allergy suffers could use it to check if dish contains any allergens.

Key challanges

The key was to create views that accurately describe the menu content with pictures of dishes and ingredients, as well as a complete set of information about nutrients, allergens and calories.

Research study

People complain about the lack of enough information on the menu and the problem with finding a food truck. They want to have all the information in one place so that they do not get distracted when ordering a meal.

Initial design concepts

When designing the homepage, I wanted the user to be able to easily search for a food truck and to have access to recommended restaurants in his area..

Wireframes

Moving from paper to digital wireframes made it easy to understand how the redesign could help address user pain points and improve the user experience. To achieve these goals I added a left filter card which allows filter results and added a place for a rich course description.

User testing results

User feedback after testing shows that there are several things that can be changed. The 'Search' button on the home page was confusing. The user expects to enter something and not redirect to the map view. Information about nutrients, weight, calories and allergens take too much place.

Mockups

I took into account the concerns of users and changed the name of the button that redirects to the map view. Instead of showing all the information that would need to be scrolled, I used the expansion panel. Thanks to this, the user can choose the most important information without unnecessary scrolling through the content. You can see final result below.

What I learned

I learned that it's worth being mindful of your prejudices. What is obvious to me as a designer on a site can be difficult for a user to change. Additionally, I learned how to design lo-fi and hi-fi prototypes in the Figma environment.

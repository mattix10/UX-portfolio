MedicineReminder App

Medicine Reminder App is an application that reminds you to take your medication at the right time. The app thus helps to stay healthy and is especially useful for the elderly.

The goal

Design an app wichich allows to control the time you take medication and thus improve your health.

Target audience

The application is dedicated to people who constantly take medications or use dietary supplements and do not want to skip doses.

Key challanges

One of the most important things was to create a simple 'user flow' for adding medications and a clear reminder. As a result, the user is not overwhelmed by the multitude of options, but can quickly create his own drug list and receive notifications when the time comes to take the drug.

Research study

People wants to see not only an image of medicine but name, dose and hour too - it was a key insight which allowed adjust notification view to user requiremenets.

Initial design concepts

Before creating digital wireframes I started from sketching some draws on paper. I wondered how to simplify the process of adding drugs to list and show them in the separated list, so I sketched 6 variations of this view and choose the best option.

Wireframes

After drawing the views on a piece of paper, the time has come to transfer them to the digital world. To do this, I used the Figma program, in which I created the views below.

User testing results

Users want to have additional information in ‘My drugs’ view. They want to know date of taking drugs so I added ‘Schedule’ header with date and hour of taking drugs. Additionally they want to see a name of drug, dose and an hour so I applied design changes.

Mockups and prototypes

The next step was preparing final mockup. After many experminets with colors, I decided to use navy blue color as accent, white color for cards, elements and light blue for backgorund.

What I learned

I learned that it's worth being mindful of your prejudices. What is obvious to me as a designer on a site can be difficult for a user to change. Additionally, I learned how to design lo-fi and hi-fi prototypes in the Figma environment.

Dancefloor Courses Website

DANCEfloor is a website where you can buy a special dance courses to learn dance online dedicated to people who have no time to go to typical dance school or this kind of school is so far from them. DANCEfloor goal is connect best dance instructors with everyone

The goal

Design Dance courses website where the user will be able to find right course and measure progress in learning by uploading video.

Target audience

People who are interested in dance and would like to learn dance but from different reasons they can’t go to standard course and choose online version.

Key challanges

Creating a design that will encourage the user to buy the course and give him all the necessary information to fully familiarize himself with the advantages of the course.

Research study

I discovered that some people would like to learn dance but they have no time or the dance school is quite far away. In the Internet you can find many dance tutorials but they are in many places and it’s hard to verify their quality and find the right one. Additionally, participating in these courses the user can’t measure the progress.

Initial design concepts

Preparing initial design concepts I took into account collecting all courses in one place and highlight recommended and most popular courses to suggest a choice for the user.

Wireframes

Moving from paper to digital wireframes made it easy to understand how the redesign could help address user pain points and improve the user experience. To achieve these goals I added a left filter card which allows filter results and added a place for a rich course description.

User testing results

Users want to go to course after finalizing transaction. Based on the insights from the usability study, I added purple button which redirect user to purchased course.

Mockups

After creating digital wireframes and testing them, I made mockups and prototype.

What I learned

I learned that interfaces should be simple for the user, clearly navigate the user to take action, and shouldn't contain too much information that can overwhelm the user.
